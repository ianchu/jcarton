Example orchidectomy report

CLINICAL DETAILS:
Right radical orchidectomy for testicular tumour.

MACROSCOPIC:
Pot labelled “right testis”: A radical orchidectomy specimen comprising testis measuring 7 x 5 x 4 cm with attached spermatic cord 7 cm long. External coverings appear mobile. Slicing the testis reveals a heterogenous soft tumour measuring up to 4 cm in size. Tumour appears confined to the testis macroscopically. Slicing the spermatic cord shows no abnormalities. Blocks: A1: cord margin, A2: base of cord, A3-10: tumour.

MICROSCOPIC:
Tumour types: Embryonal carcinoma (85%), yolk sac tumour (15%)
Maximum tumour diameter: 4 cm
Lymphovascular invasion: Yes, by embryonal carcinoma
Rete testis stromal invasion: No
Hilar soft tissue or direct spermatic cord invasion: No
Tumour at spermatic cord margin: No
Microscopic tunica vaginalis invasion: No
Intratubular germ cell neoplasia identified: Yes
TNM 7 tumour stage: pT2

DIAGNOSIS:
RIGHT RADICAL ORCHIDECTOMY: NON-SEMINOMATOUS GERM CELL TUMOUR (85% EMBRYONAL CARCINOMA, 15% YOLK SAC TUMOUR), WITH VASCULAR INVASION, STAGE pT2, EXCISED.
